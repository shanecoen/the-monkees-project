Peter Tork

Born February 13th 1942 in Washington D.C. and is the oldest member of The Monkees.

Best known as the dimpled and naive member of the legendary 60's group, Peter has played many roles in his life: musician, actor, teacher, mentor, father and husband.

Peter plays many instruments: keyboards, guitar, bass, banjo and various types of percussion and wind instruments. His first instrument was the ukelele.


Micky Dolenz

Born in Los Angeles on March 8th 1948.

Micky first established himself as a performer at age 10 when, under the stage name of Micky Braddock, he starred in his first TV series, Circus Boy, which aired on NBC from 1956-1958.

In his teens, Micky guest-starred on a number of television shows and also learned to play guitar.

Micky has spent much of his recent years acting and directing working with such shows as The Drew Carey Show and Days Of Our Lives and has also enjoyed time working in Broadway.


David Jones

Born in Manchester on December 30th 1945.

His television acting debut was on the British television soap opera Coronation Street where he appeared for just one episode in 1961.

Davy spent some of his youth as a jockey before taking up performing full time.

While working on The Monkees TV show he attracted a lot of attention from fans for his charm, wit, warm sense of humor and boyish good looks. He sang lead vocals on many of the Monkees' recordings and was known as a good drummer.


Michael Nesmith

Born in Houston, Texas on December 30th 1942.

He was a noted player of the twelve-string guitar, performing on custom-built twelve-string electric guitars with The Monkees.

He spent a lot of his youth involved in drama activites where he featured in several plays and also enjoyed writing verse poetry.

He is also an executive producer of the cult film Repo Man (1984). In 1981, Michael won the first Grammy Award given for Video of the Year for his hour-long television show, Elephant Parts.




Much More Than Just the music

The Monkees Are Born

In 1965 the search began for 4 young men to star in a new TV series with the plan of riding on the coat-tails of The Beatles phenomenon but who would have guessed that 50 years after its premiere (and two running seasons) that The Monkees would remain as popular as ever.

The Monkees was a light-hearted sitcom following the bands exploits that featured early incarnations of music videos and plenty of zanny antics. The show ran from 1966 to 1968 but gained plenty of new fans in the 1980s with the MTV generation. In all 58 episodes were aired and The Monkees rock n roll legend was born!
